Attractions

Whether you want to see some of the vast geographic areas, or tour some of the oldest points in the world, Ethiopia provides it all. There are hundreds of attractions to see, but I wanted to keylist the Top 4 that will not dissapoint you in your first visit!

The National Museum of Ethopia

If you are a history buff like me, the National Museum of Ethiopia will not dissappoint. It holds one of the oldest human ancestor remains Lucy which is a partial human skeleton, that is over 3.3 million years old! The ground floor of the building showcases the different eras in Ethiopias History spanning from the Pre-Aksumite Empire to Present Day Ethiopia.The first floor has some of the countries most famous pieces, including canvas-oil paintings of some of the Kings and Queens from the past monarchy.

The Danakil Depression

The Danakil Depression is the geologically the lowest place in Africa, which lies approximately 125 meters below sea level. The Depression was formed due to Africa and Asia seperating, causing rifting and volcanic activity. This area is a must-see, if you are interested in seeing live volcanos, hot springs, and the Yellow Lake shown above.

Lalibela

If you want to take a time machine to the medieval civilizations of Ethiopia, Lalibela is your scene! The main attraction in Lalibela is the Church of St. George, which was built in the late 12th century AD. The underground church is looked at as a marvel of construction by historians due to its articulate designs in both the interior and exterior of the building. The Church is a main attraction in the country, due to it being one of the last stone-cut monolithic churches.

The Blue Nile Falls

Comparable to the Niagara Falls, The Blue Nile Falls is a top attraction in the country. The falls have an approximate height of 37-45 meters high, which consists of four streams. The volume of water varies widely between the wet and dry seasons of Ethiopia. Historically, the surrounding areas of the area were used to produce lime stone which contributed to the early construction of infrastructure in the area.
